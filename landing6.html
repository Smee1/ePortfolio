GDPR Case Studies

GDPR case studies (Cookieyes, 2021)

1. British airways- British airways had a very public breach in 2020 which results in a £20 million fine for a data breach that actually occurred 2 years earlier. The breach affected the credit cards and personal data of 400,000 people. The exact details have not been published. It is suspected that there was malicious code on the website that extracted personal details when customers put their details in. The CVV details were also taken which are not usually stored by companies when following correct standards. By hackers taking the CVV number they can use the card for further payments and thus fraud.

2. Marriott international - Marriott international was fined by ISO due to a breach of customers' personal data. The fine was £18.4million. The breach occurred in 2014 but was not discovered until 2018. Personal data that was stolen included email addresses, names, phone numbers, unencrypted password numbers, arrival/departure information, and guests' status e.g., VIPs. This can be incredibly damaging information if in the wrong hands and can cause huge issues for customers. If they use the same password for different logins hackers could have received even more information as well. It is estimated that 7 million people were affected. The data that was breached can be very valuable on the dark web.

3. Vodafone- Vodafone 2020 was fined €12.25 million due to a GDPR fine. This was done by the Italian Data Protection Authority (Garante). They were fined due having to aggressive telemarketing practices. The Garante found that the system that stored customers' information had many issues and they had also purchased information about people from other companies without holding proper consent. Not obtaining personal information through the correct avenues this a breach of international and Italian standards which companies are expected to follow; especially large companies such as Vodafone.

Artifact

Case Study 3: Prosecutions: Marketing Offences

When considering GDPR this case study breaks core principles. The personal information was not used in the correct way. Even though personal data such as phone numbers or email addresses were collected through legal avenues the companies did not have permission to use this data in that way. This, therefore, resulted in a fine to the company for incorrect data use under GDPR law. If this was my organization, I would make sure protocols were in place so personal information was used in the correct ways. I would ensure I had regular audits with each department and make sure staff were aware of whistleblowing and how to go about doing this if it was appropriate. I would ensure all staff had annual training in GDPR so they could understand and refresh their memory. Companies must respect if a person opts out of marketing calls, texts, emails, or letters they have to respect the law and follow by this.
