Introduction to Security and Risk Standards in Industry and the Enterprise

Unit 5

Cyber security standards mandatory for the UK government

There is a Minimum cyber security standard that is mandatory for the UK government (it governance, 2012). The UK government has five cyber essentials including:

1. Boundary firewalls and Internet gateways

2. Secure configuration

3. User access control

4. Malware protection

5. Patch management

The UK government has a scheme involving a certification process that helps organizations prove to customers that they are invested in cyber security. I would ensure any future company I work for will take part in this scheme as the government has tried to keep cost lows to entice companies to take part. MCSS has created a cyber resilience outcome and can be used by any organization as a reference for creating cyber resilience (it governance, 2012). An example of a cyber security framework is PAS 555. It is a framework that considers multiple aspects of a threat including technical, physical, cultural, and Behavioural (it governance, 2012). PAS 555 is interesting as can be adapted depending on the size of the organization and is not-for-profit. There have been some international standards created including ISO 27001 (it governance, 2012). ISO 27001 takes into account processes and people as well as technology. In my future career, I will likely adapt to create my own set of standards which include the minimum requirement from the UK government and international guidelines. I would hope to add to these requirements and have a strong standard by which I work to have good cyber security. Having standards that are followed nationally and internationally can change risk assessments. By having awareness and appropriate education for teams and organizations risk assessments can change and become more specific and change the criteria to be included in risk assessments.
