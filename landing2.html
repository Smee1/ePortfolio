Unit 2

Risk management review process (RMP)

Risk management review process (RMP) is a framework which can be split into five steps (Thomas, 2022);

1. Identify1

2. Analyse

3. Evaluate

4. Treat

5. Monitor & Review

A qualitative risk assessment is most common. Qualitative is subjective whereas quantitative is factual (TrustNet, No date). Both are important in their own ways. Qualitative risk assessments needs to be thorough but also allow companies to continue to work under a standardised process (Thomas, 2022). If I worked under a future company, I would make sure the process is easy for all staff to understand with certain specialists having more detail in the different protocols. Quantitative risk assessment is important when finances are involved. This is because it is a metric process and thus objective (Thomas, 2022). An example of quantitative analysis in cyber security is factor analysis of information risk (FAIR)(Maze, 2018) Both quantitative and qualitative are important to consider in future companies. I would also make sure there was continued reflective process so lessons can be learnt in a safe environment.
