Quantitative risk modelling (QRM) is when an organization quantifies and manages its risk. It is a scientific approach which is needed to be carried out to fulfil stakeholders' concerns. Carrying out QRM projects can reach budgets and be more effective (Herold financial dictionary, No Date). An example for QRM being used is to estimate the probability of risk with cash flow. QRM works by using a set of conceptual frameworks, tools, and practices that come together to create a technique to assess risk (Open risk manual, No date). Bayes and Monte Carlo simulations are types of QRM. Bayesian is a probability statistical approach in which the parameters are quantified using data observed (UNSW, No date). They are both practical analytic strategies but Monte Carlo takes the parameters from a previous data source (McCAndles & Gustafson, 2017). Monte Carlo may give larger intervals of probability when compared to Bayes (McCAndles & Gustafson, 2017).
